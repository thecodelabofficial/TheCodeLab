Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What will students learn?

Students will start with Scratch, then move on to Python, and later choose between Java or C++.

2. How long are the classes?

Each class is 3 hours long, and there are two classes per week.

3. Will there be homework or projects?

No homework or outside projects are required. However, we encourage students to explore and review class materials on their own time.

4. Do students need prior experience?

No prior experience is required for beginners. However, students who want to join Level 2 must have completed Level 1.

5. Is there a makeup policy?

There are no makeup classes for absences. However, class materials will be uploaded to our WhatsApp group for review.

6. Are classes in person or online?

All classes are in-person and held after school.

7. How many students per class?

Each class has a maximum of 20 students, with volunteers assisting the main instructor.

8. What should students know before joining?

We recommend students know basic computer skills — how to turn on a computer, use the mouse, type, open websites, and understand basic English, since coding uses English words.

9. Will there be more advanced levels?

Yes! This is our first year, and we are already designing advanced levels for students who want to continue.

10. What do you look for in teachers or volunteers?

We look for people with experience engaging with kids or basic coding knowledge.

11. What is the pay for teachers?

Pay ranges from RM10–RM16 per hour, depending on experience.

12. Do you provide training and support for teachers?

Yes, we provide training and full curriculum support for all new teachers and volunteers.

13. Do parents get progress reports?

Yes. Parents will receive a progress report after every class.

14. Do you offer English teaching options?

Yes, we also provide English learning options alongside coding classes.

About TheCodeLab

Why Choose TheCodeLab?

In today's rapidly evolving world, AI and technology are reshaping every aspect of our lives. From the apps we use daily to the careers of tomorrow, coding has become an essential skill—not just for programmers, but for everyone who wants to understand and thrive in our digital future.

Our Approach: Happy Learning Through Games

At TheCodeLab, we believe learning should be joyful, not stressful. We don't force kids into rigid learning structures. Instead, we use games, creative projects, and hands-on activities to make coding fun and engaging. When children enjoy what they're learning, they naturally become more curious, creative, and confident problem-solvers.

We actively encourage more girls and young women to join our programs. 💡 The future of technology needs diverse voices. We're committed to making coding accessible and welcoming to all students, regardless of gender or background.
